Les Temps Verbaux en Anglais

Maîtriser le système temporel pour une expression précise et nuancée
Point essentiel Niveau Terminale

📌 Introduction

Le système des temps verbaux en anglais est plus complexe qu'en français, avec 12 temps principaux qui permettent d'exprimer avec précision quand une action se déroule et comment elle se rapporte à d'autres actions. La maîtrise de ces temps est essentielle pour communiquer efficacement en anglais.

1. Les temps du présent

Present Simple

Formation et structure

Affirmatif
Sujet + verbe (+ -s à la 3e personne du singulier)
I work. / She works.
Négatif
Sujet + do/does + not + verbe
I do not (don't) work. / She does not (doesn't) work.
Interrogatif
Do/Does + sujet + verbe
Do you work? / Does she work?
Points d'attention
  • Ajout de -s ou -es à la 3e personne du singulier (he, she, it)
  • Verbes se terminant par -y : le y devient i avant d'ajouter -es (study → studies)
  • Verbes irréguliers : have → has, do → does

Utilisations

🔄
Habitudes et routines
I get up at 7am every day.
She drinks coffee for breakfast.
📊
Faits généraux et vérités permanentes
Water boils at 100°C.
The Earth revolves around the Sun.
📅
Horaires et programmes
The train leaves at 5pm.
The concert starts at 8pm tomorrow.
📖
Instructions et récits
First, you add the flour, then you mix it with water.
In the novel, the hero discovers a secret passage.
Marqueurs temporels fréquents
always usually often sometimes rarely never every day/week/month/year once a week on Mondays
Exercice rapide

Complétez les phrases avec la forme correcte du Present Simple :

1. My sister (to study) __________ medicine at university.
My sister studies medicine at university.
2. They (not/to live) __________ in London.
They don't live in London.
3. (you/to play) __________ any musical instruments?
Do you play any musical instruments?

Present Continuous

Formation et structure

Affirmatif
Sujet + be (am/is/are) + verbe-ing
I am working. / She is working. / They are working.
Négatif
Sujet + be + not + verbe-ing
I am not (I'm not) working. / She is not (isn't) working.
Interrogatif
Be + sujet + verbe-ing
Am I working? / Is she working? / Are they working?
Règles orthographiques pour -ing
  • Généralement, on ajoute simplement -ing : work → working
  • Si le verbe se termine par -e, on supprime le e : write → writing
  • Si le verbe se termine par consonne + voyelle + consonne, on double la dernière consonne : run → running
  • Si le verbe se termine par -ie, on change ie en y : lie → lying

Utilisations

⏳
Actions en cours au moment présent
I am studying right now.
Look! It is raining.
📆
Actions temporaires
I am staying with my parents until I find an apartment.
She is working in London for three months.
🔜
Actions planifiées dans un futur proche
I am meeting John tomorrow.
We are flying to Paris next week.
😤
Actions répétées avec une connotation d'irritation
He is always forgetting his keys.
They are constantly complaining about the weather.
Marqueurs temporels fréquents
now at the moment right now currently these days this week/month today tomorrow next week
Verbes rarement utilisés au continuous

Certains verbes, appelés "verbes d'état", sont rarement utilisés à la forme progressive :

Sentiments et émotions
like love hate prefer want
Pensée et opinion
think believe understand know remember
Perception
see hear smell taste
Possession
have own possess belong
✓ I think she is right.
✗ I am thinking she is right.
Note : Certains de ces verbes peuvent être utilisés au continuous avec un sens différent.
I think Paris is beautiful. (opinion)
I am thinking about my next holiday. (réfléchir à)
Exercice rapide

Complétez les phrases avec la forme correcte du Present Continuous :

1. Shh! The baby (to sleep) __________.
Shh! The baby is sleeping.
2. What (you/to do) __________ this weekend?
What are you doing this weekend?
3. They (not/to work) __________ today because it's a holiday.
They aren't working today because it's a holiday.

Exercice de discrimination

Choisissez le temps verbal approprié :

1. Look! The bus (comes / is coming) __________.
Look! The bus is coming.
2. The Earth (goes / is going) __________ around the Sun.
The Earth goes around the Sun.
3. She usually (walks / is walking) __________ to school, but today she (takes / is taking) __________ the bus.

Present Perfect

Formation et structure

Affirmatif
Sujet + have/has + participe passé
I have worked. / She has worked.
Négatif
Sujet + have/has + not + participe passé
I have not (haven't) worked. / She has not (hasn't) worked.
Interrogatif
Have/Has + sujet + participe passé
Have you worked? / Has she worked?
Participe passé

Pour les verbes réguliers : infinitif + -ed (work → worked)

Pour les verbes irréguliers : forme spécifique à apprendre (go → gone, see → seen)

Regular: I have worked, played, listened
Irregular: I have gone, seen, done, been

Utilisations

🔄
Expériences passées sans précision de temps
I have visited Paris. (à un moment indéterminé dans le passé)
Have you ever eaten sushi?
⏱️
Actions récentes avec impact sur le présent
I have lost my keys. (et je ne peux pas entrer chez moi maintenant)
She has broken her leg. (et elle est maintenant en plâtre)
📈
Actions commencées dans le passé et qui continuent
I have lived here for ten years. (et j'y vis toujours)
She has worked at this company since 2015.
✅
Actions achevées dans une période non terminée
I have read three books this month. (le mois n'est pas encore terminé)
She has visited Paris twice this year.
Marqueurs temporels fréquents
just already yet ever never for since recently lately so far this week/month/year
Différence entre "for" et "since"
FOR
SINCE
Indique une durée
for two hours
for six months
for a long time
Indique un point de départ
since 2010
since Monday
since I was a child
Exercice rapide

Complétez les phrases avec la forme correcte du Present Perfect :

1. I (to see) __________ that film three times.
I have seen that film three times.
2. She (not/to finish) __________ her homework yet.
She hasn't finished her homework yet.
3. How long (you/to know) __________ each other?
How long have you known each other?

Present Perfect Continuous

Formation et structure

Affirmatif
Sujet + have/has + been + verbe-ing
I have been working. / She has been working.
Négatif
Sujet + have/has + not + been + verbe-ing
I have not (haven't) been working. / She has not (hasn't) been working.
Interrogatif
Have/Has + sujet + been + verbe-ing
Have you been working? / Has she been working?

Utilisations

⏳
Actions continues sur une période jusqu'au présent
I have been studying for three hours. (et je continue d'étudier)
She has been working here since January.
💦
Actions récentes avec résultats visibles
I'm tired because I have been running.
Why are your clothes wet? — I have been washing the car.
🔄
Actions répétées sur une période
She has been calling him all day.
They have been fighting a lot recently.
😤
Situations irritantes ou qui dérangent
Someone has been using my computer without permission.
The neighbors have been making noise all night.
Marqueurs temporels fréquents
for since all day/week recently lately how long
Present Perfect vs. Present Perfect Continuous
Present Perfect
Present Perfect Continuous
Accent sur le résultat
I have written three essays.
(l'accent est mis sur le nombre d'essais terminés)
Accent sur l'activité
I have been writing essays all day.
(l'accent est mis sur l'activité d'écriture)
Action complétée
She has cleaned the house.
(la maison est maintenant propre)
Action en cours ou récente
She has been cleaning the house.
(elle est peut-être encore en train de nettoyer)
Utilisé avec les verbes d'état
I have known him for years.
Rarement utilisé avec les verbes d'état
I have been knowing him for years.
Exercice rapide

Complétez les phrases avec la forme correcte du Present Perfect Continuous :

1. I (to wait) __________ for you for over an hour!
I have been waiting for you for over an hour!
2. She (to learn) __________ French since last year.
She has been learning French since last year.
3. They (not/to work) __________ very hard lately.
They haven't been working very hard lately.

2. Les temps du passé

Past Simple

Formation et structure

Affirmatif
Sujet + verbe au prétérit
I worked. / She worked. / They went.
Négatif
Sujet + did + not + infinitif
I did not (didn't) work. / She did not (didn't) go.
Interrogatif
Did + sujet + infinitif
Did you work? / Did she go?
Formation du prétérit
Verbes réguliers

Infinitif + -ed

work → worked
play → played

Règles orthographiques :

  • Verbe terminé par -e : ajouter -d (like → liked)
  • Consonne + voyelle + consonne : doubler la dernière consonne (stop → stopped)
  • Verbe terminé par consonne + y : y → i + ed (study → studied)
Verbes irréguliers

Forme spécifique à apprendre

go → went
see → saw
have → had
do → did

Utilisations

📅
Actions terminées à un moment précis du passé
I visited Paris last summer.
She graduated in 2018.
🔄
Habitudes ou états dans le passé
When I was a child, I played football every day.
She lived in London for ten years.
📚
Récits, histoires et événements successifs
He entered the room, looked around and sat down.
The Romans invaded Britain in 43 AD.
🔚
États ou situations qui ne sont plus vrais
I had a dog when I was young. (je n'ai plus de chien maintenant)
She worked at a bank before she retired.
Marqueurs temporels fréquents
yesterday last week/month/year in 2010 on Monday ago when then once
Exercice rapide

Complétez les phrases avec la forme correcte du Past Simple :

1. We (to go) __________ to the cinema last night.
We went to the cinema last night.
2. She (not/to like) __________ the film very much.
She didn't like the film very much.
3. (you/to finish) __________ your homework yesterday?
Did you finish your homework yesterday?

Past Continuous

Formation et structure

Affirmatif
Sujet + was/were + verbe-ing
I was working. / She was working. / They were working.
Négatif
Sujet + was/were + not + verbe-ing
I was not (wasn't) working. / They were not (weren't) working.
Interrogatif
Was/Were + sujet + verbe-ing
Was I working? / Were they working?

Utilisations

⏳
Actions en cours à un moment précis du passé
At 8pm last night, I was watching TV.
When you called, she was sleeping.
🔄
Actions en cours interrompues par une autre action
I was taking a shower when the phone rang.
While she was cooking, someone knocked at the door.
📆
Actions temporaires ou en cours pendant une période du passé
Last year, I was working in Madrid.
They were living in Paris during the 1990s.
🔄
Actions parallèles dans le passé
While I was studying, my sister was watching TV.
The children were playing while their parents were preparing dinner.
Marqueurs temporels fréquents
at 8pm yesterday when while as all day/morning/evening at that time during
Past Simple vs. Past Continuous
Past Simple
Past Continuous
Actions complètes
I read a book yesterday.
(action terminée)
Actions en cours
I was reading a book at 10pm.
(action en cours à ce moment-là)
Séquence d'événements
She entered the room and sat down.
Contexte ou arrière-plan
She was watching TV when I arrived.
Action qui interrompt
The phone rang while I was cooking.
Action interrompue
I was cooking when the phone rang.
Exercice rapide

Complétez les phrases avec la forme correcte du Past Continuous :

1. At 3pm yesterday, I (to have) __________ lunch with a client.
At 3pm yesterday, I was having lunch with a client.
2. While she (to drive) __________ to work, she saw an accident.
While she was driving to work, she saw an accident.
3. They (not/to listen) __________ when the teacher explained the exercise.
They weren't listening when the teacher explained the exercise.

Past Perfect

Formation et structure

Affirmatif
Sujet + had + participe passé
I had worked. / She had worked.
Négatif
Sujet + had + not + participe passé
I had not (hadn't) worked. / She had not (hadn't) worked.
Interrogatif
Had + sujet + participe passé
Had you worked? / Had she worked?

Utilisations

⏪
Actions antérieures à un moment du passé
When I arrived, the film had already started. (le film a commencé avant mon arrivée)
She had finished her homework before dinner.
🔄
Expériences antérieures à un moment du passé
I had never seen snow before I moved to Canada.
By the time I was 20, I had visited five countries.
❌
Actions non réalisées avant un moment du passé
I was hungry because I hadn't eaten all day.
She failed the exam because she hadn't studied enough.
📚
Discours indirect pour des actions au passé
She said she had seen that movie before. (discours direct : "I have seen that movie before.")
He told me he had never been to Paris.
Marqueurs temporels fréquents
before after already just never by the time until then previously
Représentation chronologique
Past Perfect
had finished
Past Simple
arrived
Present
now

Le Past Perfect exprime une action qui s'est produite avant une autre action au passé (exprimée généralement au Past Simple).

Exercice rapide

Complétez les phrases avec la forme correcte du Past Perfect :

1. By the time we arrived, the concert (to start) __________.
By the time we arrived, the concert had started.
2. She couldn't find her keys because she (to lose) __________ them.
She couldn't find her keys because she had lost them.
3. When I got to the station, the train (already/to leave) __________.
When I got to the station, the train had already left.

Formation et structure

Affirmatif
Sujet + had + been + verbe-ing
I had been working. / She had been working.
Négatif
Sujet + had + not + been + verbe-ing
I had not (hadn't) been working. / She had not (hadn't) been working.
Interrogatif
Had + sujet + been + verbe-ing
Had you been working? / Had she been working?

Utilisations

⏳
Actions continues jusqu'à un moment du passé
I had been working for three hours when she called.
They had been living in Paris for ten years before they moved to London.
💦
Actions passées avec résultats visibles dans le passé

Her eyes were red because she had been crying.
🔄
Actions répétées jusqu'à un moment du passé
The neighbors had been complaining about the noise for weeks before the police intervened.
She had been trying to contact him for days when he finally called back.
❓
Explications de situations passées
I was tired because I had been studying all night.
The ground was wet. It had been raining.
Marqueurs temporels fréquents
for since how long all day/week before when until then

Past Perfect
Past Perfect Continuous
Accent sur le résultat
By 2010, she had written three novels.
(l'accent est mis sur le nombre de romans terminés)

3. Les temps du futur

Future Simple (will)

Formation et structure

Affirmatif
Sujet + will + infinitif
I will work. / She will work.
Négatif
Sujet + will + not + infinitif
I will not (won't) work. / She will not (won't) work.
Interrogatif
Will + sujet + infinitif
Will you work? / Will she work?

Utilisations

🔮
Prédictions et faits futurs
I think it will rain tomorrow.
The population will reach 10 billion by 2050.
💭
Décisions spontanées
I'm thirsty. I will get a glass of water. (décision prise au moment où l'on parle)
The phone is ringing. I will answer it.
🤝
Promesses et offres
I will help you with your homework.
Don't worry, I won't tell anyone your secret.
❓
Demandes et questions
Will you close the window, please?
Will you be at the meeting tomorrow?
Marqueurs temporels fréquents
tomorrow next week/month/year in the future soon in 2030 one day probably I think
Exercice rapide

Complétez les phrases avec la forme correcte du Future Simple :

1. I think it (to snow) __________ tomorrow.
I think it will snow tomorrow.
2. Don't worry, I (not/to forget) __________ your birthday.
Don't worry, I won't forget your birthday.
3. (you/to help) __________ me with this heavy bag?
Will you help me with this heavy bag?

Be Going To

Formation et structure

Affirmatif
Sujet + be (am/is/are) + going to + infinitif
I am going to work. / She is going to work. / They are going to work.
Négatif
Sujet + be + not + going to + infinitif
I am not going to work. / She is not (isn't) going to work.
Interrogatif
Be + sujet + going to + infinitif
Am I going to work? / Is she going to work? / Are they going to work?

Utilisations

📝
Intentions et plans futurs
I am going to study medicine at university. (intention ou plan déjà décidé)
They are going to get married next year.
🔍
Prédictions basées sur des preuves visibles
Look at those clouds! It is going to rain. (on peut voir les signes de la pluie)
Watch out! That tree is going to fall!
🔜
Actions imminentes
The train is going to leave in five minutes.
Be careful! You are going to spill your coffee!
❌
Actions qu'on a l'intention de ne pas faire
I am not going to tell you the secret.
She is not going to apply for that job.
Marqueurs temporels fréquents
soon this weekend tomorrow next week/month/year in the near future
Will vs. Be Going To
WILL
BE GOING TO
Prédictions générales

(opinion, prédiction sans preuve visible)
Prédictions avec preuves

(prédiction basée sur des signes visibles)
Décisions spontanées
I'm thirsty. I will get a glass of water.
(décision prise au moment où l'on parle)
Plans et intentions

(plan déjà décidé avant le moment de parole)

4. Autres formes verbales importantes

Present Perfect vs. Past Simple

Comparaison essentielle

La distinction entre le Present Perfect et le Past Simple est l'une des plus difficiles pour les francophones, car ces deux temps peuvent souvent se traduire par le passé composé en français.

Autres façons d'exprimer le futur

Present Continuous pour le futur

I am meeting John tomorrow. (arrangement fixé à l'avance)
We are flying to Paris next week. (plan organisé)

Present Simple pour le futur

The train leaves at 5pm tomorrow. (horaire fixe)
The semester starts next Monday. (événement programmé)
I was about to call you when you arrived. (action sur le point de se produire dans le passé)

Be to + infinitif

The President is to visit France next month. (arrangement officiel)
You are to stay here until I return. (instruction ou ordre)

Be due to + infinitif

The train is due to arrive at 3pm. (prévu selon un horaire)
The report is due to be published next week. (prévu pour une date précise)

Used to et Would

Used to + infinitif

Exprime des habitudes ou des états dans le passé qui ne sont plus vrais maintenant.

Affirmatif
Sujet + used to + infinitif
I used to play tennis. / She used to live in Paris.
Négatif
Sujet + didn't + use to + infinitif
I didn't use to like coffee. / She didn't use to work here.
Interrogatif
Did + sujet + use to + infinitif
Did you use to play sports? / Did she use to live there?
I used to smoke, but I quit five years ago. (habitude passée qui n'existe plus)
She used to have long hair when she was younger. (état passé qui a changé)
I didn't use to like classical music, but now I do. (goût qui a changé)

Would + infinitif

Exprime des habitudes répétées dans le passé (mais pas des états).

When I was a child, I would visit my grandparents every Sunday. (habitude répétée dans le passé)
She would always bring flowers when she came to dinner. (action habituelle dans le passé)
I would have long hair when I was younger. (incorrect pour un état - utilisez "used to have")
Used to vs. Would
Used to
Would
Habitudes et états passés
I used to play football.
I used to be shy.
Seulement habitudes passées (pas d'états)
I would play football every weekend.
I would be shy. ✗
Contraste clair avec le présent
I used to smoke, but now I don't.
Accent sur la répétition
Whenever it rained, we would stay indoors.
Peut commencer un récit
I used to live in a small village...
Généralement dans un contexte narratif
When I was young, we would go to the beach...
Exercice rapide

Complétez les phrases avec "used to" ou "would" selon le contexte :

I used to be afraid of the dark when I was a child.
2. Every Saturday, we __________ go fishing with my father.
Every Saturday, we would go fishing with my father.
3. She __________ have blonde hair before she dyed it brown.
She used to have blonde hair before she dyed it brown.
4. Whenever I visited her, she __________ make me a cup of tea.
Whenever I visited her, she would make me a cup of tea.

Exercice 1 : Choisir le temps approprié

Complétez les phrases avec le temps verbal qui convient :

1. I __________ (wait) for the bus when it started to rain.
I was waiting for the bus when it started to rain.
2. By the time we reach Paris, we __________ (drive) for more than ten hours.
By the time we reach Paris, we will have been driving for more than ten hours.
3. She __________ (live) in London since 2015.
She has been living in London since 2015.
4. I __________ (see) this film three times already.
I have seen this film three times already.
5. Look at those clouds! It __________ (rain) soon.
Look at those clouds! It is going to rain soon.
Exercice 2 : Narration au passé

Complétez le texte avec les temps du passé appropriés :

Last summer, I (visit) my grandparents in the countryside. When I (arrive), my grandmother (prepare) lunch. She (cook) all morning. My grandfather (work) in the garden since dawn and (be) very tired. After lunch, while my grandmother (wash) the dishes, I (help) my grandfather in the garden. Before I (come) to visit them, they (plan) to renovate their house.

Last summer, I visited my grandparents in the countryside. When I arrived, my grandmother was preparing lunch. She had been cooking all morning. My grandfather had been working in the garden since dawn and was very tired. After lunch, while my grandmother was washing the dishes, I helped my grandfather in the garden. Before I came to visit them, they had planned to renovate their house.

Exercice 3 : Projets futurs

Complétez le texte avec les temps du futur appropriés :

Next year, I (study) abroad. My plane (leave) on September 15th at 10am. By the time I arrive, I (learn) some basic phrases in the local language. Next year, I am going to study abroad. My plane leaves on September 15th at 10am. By the time I arrive, I will have learned some basic phrases in the local language. Erreurs fréquentes à éviter
  • Confusion entre Present Perfect et Past Simple
  • Oubli du -s à la 3e personne du singulier au Present Simple
  • Mauvaise formation des verbes irréguliers
  • Utilisation incorrecte des marqueurs temporels

Conclusion

La maîtrise des temps verbaux en anglais est essentielle pour communiquer avec précision et nuance. Chaque temps verbal permet d'exprimer une relation temporelle spécifique et de situer les actions dans le temps.

Pour progresser :

  • Pratiquez régulièrement en identifiant les temps dans vos lectures
  • Entraînez-vous à utiliser une variété de temps dans vos productions écrites et orales
  • Soyez attentifs aux marqueurs temporels qui accompagnent les différents temps
  • Révisez les verbes irréguliers et les structures spécifiques à chaque temps
