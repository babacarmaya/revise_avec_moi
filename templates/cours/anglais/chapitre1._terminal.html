1. Present Tenses Mastery

Present Simple

Basic Form

Formation

Affirmative:

Subject + V(base form/s)

I work / She works
Negative:

Subject + don't/doesn't + V(base)

I don't work / She doesn't work
Question:

Do/Does + Subject + V(base)

Do you work? / Does she work?

Key Uses

1. Habits & Routines

I wake up at 6 AM every day.

She always drinks coffee in the morning.

2. General Truths

The sun rises in the east.

Water boils at 100°C.

3. Fixed Arrangements

The train leaves at 8 PM.

The semester starts next week.

Common Time Markers

always usually often sometimes rarely never every day once a week

Practice Exercises

1. Fill in the gaps with the correct form

1. My brother _____ (study) medicine at university.

Correct answer: "studies"

We use -s in the third person singular (he/she/it)

2. Error Correction

Find and correct the errors in these sentences:

She don't like coffee.

Correct: She doesn't like coffee.

Explanation: Use "doesn't" for third person singular negative

Present Continuous

Progressive Form

Formation

Affirmative:

Subject + am/is/are + V(ing)

I am working / She is working
Negative:

Subject + am/is/are + not + V(ing)

I am not working / She isn't working
Question:

Am/Is/Are + Subject + V(ing)

Are you working? / Is she working?

Spelling Rules for -ing form

Basic Rule

Add -ing: work → working

Verbs ending in -e

Remove -e and add -ing: write → writing

Short vowel + consonant

Double final consonant: run → running

Key Uses

1. Actions happening now

I am studying English right now.

They are playing football at the moment.

2. Temporary situations

I am living with my aunt until I find an apartment.

She is working on a new project this month.

3. Future arrangements

I am meeting John tomorrow.

We are flying to Paris next week.

Present Perfect

Perfect Form

Formation

Affirmative:

Subject + have/has + V(past participle)

I have worked / She has worked
Negative:

Subject + have/has + not + V(past participle)

I haven't worked / She hasn't worked
Question:

Have/Has + Subject + V(past participle)

Have you worked? / Has she worked?

Common Irregular Past Participles

go → gone do → done see → seen
write → written speak → spoken take → taken

Key Uses

1. Past experiences

I have visited Paris three times.

She has never eaten sushi.

2. Recent past

I have just finished my homework.

They have already left.

3. Unfinished time periods

I have studied a lot this week.

She has worked hard this year.

Time Markers for Present Perfect

just already yet ever never since for lately

Past Tenses

Past Simple

Basic Past

Past Tenses Overview

Past Simple

Structure: Subject + V(ed/irregular)

  • Regular: work → worked
  • Irregular: go → went

Used for:

  • Completed past actions
  • Past habits
  • Sequential events

Past Continuous

Structure: was/were + V(ing)

Used for:

  • Actions in progress in the past
  • Interrupted actions
  • Parallel past actions

Future Forms

Will

Structure: will + V(base)

  • Predictions
  • Spontaneous decisions
  • Promises

Going to

Structure: be + going to + V(base)

  • Planned actions
  • Evidence-based predictions

Common Irregular Verbs

go → went → gone
do → did → done
make → made → made
see → saw → seen
come → came → come

Advanced Grammar Points

Perfect Continuous Tenses

Tense Structure Example
Present Perfect Continuous have/has been + V(ing) I have been studying
Past Perfect Continuous had been + V(ing) I had been working

Conditionals (Important for Bac)

Zero Conditional

If + present simple, present simple

If you heat water, it boils.

First Conditional

If + present simple, will + infinitive

If it rains, I will stay home.

Second Conditional

If + past simple, would + infinitive

If I had money, I would travel.

Third Conditional

If + past perfect, would have + past participle

If I had studied, I would have passed.

Bac Exam Preparation

Typical Exam Questions

1. Tense Recognition
Identify the tense: "She has been working here since 2010."
Answer: Present Perfect Continuous
2. Tense Transformation
Change to past: "I am studying English."
Answer: I was studying English.

Exam Success Tips

  • Always check time markers (yesterday, since, for, etc.)
  • Pay attention to irregular verbs
  • Look for signal words that indicate the tense
  • Double-check subject-verb agreement
